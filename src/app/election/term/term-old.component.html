Winner selection and the Rules for User Participation: (i) once the election results are declared in each of the States [UP, Uttarakhand, Goa and Punjab], a deviation score and the rank shall be displayed for each Participant; (ii) Deviation score (D.Score) for each Participant is calculated based on how much a Participant’s valid entry deviated from the actual results. Illustration: say a Participant submitted that a Political Party “A” shall win 100 seats in Zone “X” and that Party actually won 94 seats during the elections, his/her Deviation Score shall be 6 (difference between 100 and 94) for Party “A” in Zone “X”. Similarly, deviation scores for all parties in that zone e.g. Party “B”, Party “C” will be added up and the Deviation Score for that zone will be calculated; (iii) In case of the Uttarakhand, Goa and Punjab which do not have specific zones, the Deviation Score for the entire State shall be calculated based on the submissions/entries made for each election party of that State; (iv) To win, a Participant should have a Deviation Score as close to 0 as possible. The person with the lowest Deviation Score shall be the Winner; (v) In the event of a tie amongst multiple Participants, a computerized draw of lots using a Random Number Generator shall be conducted.
